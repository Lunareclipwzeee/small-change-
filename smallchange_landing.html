Every pattern from 50 years of trading psychology research. Mark Douglas. Brett Steenbarger. Alexander Elder. Jesse Livermore.

01
Revenge Trading
After a loss, the brain seeks recovery immediately. The next trade is emotional not logical. Almost always a larger loss.
A loss is feedback, not a signal to recover. - Mark Douglas
DETECTED BY: Time since last loss and rapid trade placement
HIGH RISK
02
FOMO Trading
Fast price movement triggers fear of missing out. Trader jumps in without a setup at the worst entry. Move is already 80% done.
The market will always have another move. - Trading in the Zone
DETECTED BY: Rapid clicking and no setup in journal
HIGH RISK
03
Tilt State
Three consecutive losses trigger survival mode. Rational brain goes offline. All rules abandoned. Accounts destroyed in hours.
Cut your size in half. Then cut it in half again. - Paul Tudor Jones
DETECTED BY: 3 losses in journal and continued trading
HIGH RISK
04
Hope Trading
Losing position held too long. Stop loss moved or ignored. The most expensive thought in trading. The account killer.
Hope is not a strategy. A stop loss is. - Jesse Livermore
DETECTED BY: Journal shows SL moved or trade held beyond plan
HIGH RISK
05
Overconfidence
After a winning streak, risk management is abandoned. The market humbles the overconfident with perfect timing.

DETECTED BY: 3 wins and neuro confidence score above 8

06
Overtrading
Decision quality degrades after every additional trade. By trade 5 it is pure gambling with a trading account.
